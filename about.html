About Us

This is the best University in East Africa.

Established in 1922 as a humble technical school, Makerere University is one of the oldest and most prestigious Universities in Africa. In January of that year, the school, which was later renamed Uganda Technical College, opened its doors to 14 day students who began studying Carpentry, Building and Mechanics. The College soon began offering various other courses in Medical Care, Agriculture , Veterinary Sciences and Teacher Training. It expanded over the years to become a Center for Higher Education in East Africa in 1935. In 1937, the College started developing into an institution of higher education, offering post-school certificate courses.

At its 148th meeting held on 15th June 2020, Makerere University Council approved the Strategic Plan (2020-2030) for Makerere University.The Stategic Plan seeks to transform Makerere University into a more research-intensive University and enhance relevance to the community and consolidate Makerere's position as Uganda's engine of development.

Vice-Chancellor.

In October we will launch the celebrations to mark 100 years of Makerere’s excellent service to humanity. The year-long celebrations will be capped by the climax in October next year. Celebrations will be held in different parts of the Country to pay tribute to all the people of Uganda, who have helped build the University to its current prestigious position as one of the best universities in Africa and the World, as well as in other capitals and cities of the World, where our more than 300,000 living alumni live and work. Makerere currently hosts students from 38 African countries, making ours a truly pan-African international university. I call upon all alumni and friends of Makerere to actively participate in these celebrations.
Soon we will launch the fundraising campaign to raise funds for the restoration of our iconic Main Administration Building, the icon of higher education in the entire east and central Africa Region, which was destroyed by a fire in September last year. Our plan is to restore the building in time for celebration of 100 years in October next year. I call upon all our alumni and friends of Makerere to contribute generously when called upon. We must raise UGX 15 billion, which is required to restore the building.
Prof. Barnabas Nawangwe, Vice-Chancellor
